Feeding India API

Feeding India is a social enterprise which aims at tackling two rampant and interconnected problems in India - food wastage and hunger. This is done by helping the needy get access to excess cooked food from restaurants and caterers.

50% of the country's children are chronically malnourished and close to 1.3 million of these die every year. More than 250 million people in India go to bed hungry each day and hunger kills more people than AIDS, Malaria, and Tuberculosis combined.

40% of food in India goes waste. Food worth $ 10,000,000 USD is wasted on a yearly basis. This is dumped in landfills, producing methane and ultimately leading to ozone layer depletion. This wasted food is enough to feed one third of the poor.

Handling donations over the phone from all over the country proved tedious and time consuming. It also meant a lot of donations couldn't be collected due to a communication gap.

I created a RESTful API that would allow users to sign up and save their address and other details. When users wish to make a donation, all they have to do is click a picture of the food they want to donate and enter the pick-up time. They can either choose their default address for pick-up or send their GPS location. This enables a guest at a party to donate food as well.

Additional parameters like food type, and packaging can also be selected.

The Android app was made by Aldrich Mascarenhas. You can download it from the Google Play Store.

  • Project type: Pro bono
  • Source: GitHub
  • Date: October 2015
  • Service: Back-End Web Developer

Arcade Game
